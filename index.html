About

Ankit Sanjyal is an AI researcher and data scientist pursuing a Master's in Data Science at Fordham University, specializing in generative modeling, diffusion methods, and vision-based learning systems.

His research focuses primarily on diffusion models and their applications in active learning, medical imaging, and vision-language integration. He is particularly interested in advancing methodologies such as no-propagation diffusion, structure-guided generation, and self-supervised representation learning, while leveraging foundational models like CLIP, DINO, and LLMs to improve robustness and generalization in real-world domains.

Alongside his work on diffusion, Ankit also explores 3D vision, including NeRF-based scene analysis and sparse-view MRI reconstruction, with the aim of improving efficiency and interpretability in high-dimensional medical and scientific imaging tasks.

Beyond model development, Ankit is committed to building modular, reproducible, and scalable research pipelines that bridge theory and practice—whether through new diffusion architectures, interpretable medical imaging frameworks, or open-source toolkits. His broader goal is to enable accessible and reliable AI for critical applications in healthcare, education, and human-AI collaboration.

Publications

RectifiedHR: High-Resolution Diffusion via Energy Profiling and Adaptive Guidance Scheduling

Ankit Sanjyal

arXiv preprint arXiv:2507.09441, 2024

A research project focused on improving high-resolution image quality generated by latent diffusion models through energy profiling and adaptive guidance scheduling. The work analyzes latent energy evolution across denoising steps and proposes rectified/adaptive guidance schedules for producing visually and quantitatively better high-res images.

Paper Code

Local Prompt Adaptation for Style-Consistent Multi-Object Generation in Diffusion Models

Ankit Sanjyal

arXiv preprint arXiv:2507.20094, 2024

Research on local style diffusion models that enable fine-grained control over image generation by applying style transformations to specific regions while maintaining global coherence. This work explores techniques for localized style transfer and region-aware diffusion processes.

NeRF Few-Shot Limitations: Analysis of Neural Radiance Fields in Low-Data Scenarios

Ankit Sanjyal

arXiv preprint arXiv:2506.18208, 2024

Investigation into the limitations and challenges of Neural Radiance Fields (NeRF) when trained with limited training data. This research analyzes the performance degradation, generalization issues, and potential solutions for few-shot NeRF training scenarios.
